# Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation (Defence-related Claims) Act 1988 (Demo Excerpt)

This is a simplified demonstration excerpt of key provisions from the Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation (Defence-related Claims) Act 1988 (Cth), commonly known as DEFGLIS.

## Section 14 - Eligibility for Compensation

A claim for compensation is eligible where the injury or disease was sustained during, or arose from, defence service and the member was on duty at the time of injury.

## Section 19 - Minimum Service Period

To be eligible for rehabilitation benefits, a member must have completed at least **90 days** of continuous defence service prior to the injury or disease onset.

## Section 24(1) - Weekly Compensation Rate

Weekly compensation shall be calculated at **85%** of the member's normal weekly earnings at the time of injury, subject to maximum limits.

## Section 27 - Medical Treatment Cap

Medical treatment costs exceeding **$5,000** per claim require prior approval from the rehabilitation authority.

## Section 68 - Permanent Impairment Threshold

Permanent impairment compensation is payable where the impairment assessment reaches or exceeds **10 impairment points** under the approved Guide to Assessment.

## Calculation Standards

All monetary calculations involving compensation payments shall use **round-half-up** methodology to **2 decimal places**, consistent with Australian Government payment standards.

**Note**: This is a demonstration excerpt. The actual DEFGLIS Act is more comprehensive. Consult the official Commonwealth legislation for complete details.
