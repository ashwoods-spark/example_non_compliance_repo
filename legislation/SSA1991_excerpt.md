# Social Security Act 1991 (Demo Excerpt)

This is a simplified demonstration excerpt of key provisions from the Social Security Act 1991 (Cth) relevant to age pension eligibility and calculation.

## Section 35(2) - Senior Rate Threshold

Individuals aged **65 years or older** qualify for the senior benefit rate category.

## Section 12(3) - Residency Requirement

To be eligible for age pension benefits, an applicant must have been an Australian resident for at least **10 months** immediately prior to application.

## Section 40 - Income Test (Prescribed Amount)

Where an applicant's annual income exceeds the prescribed amount of **AUD $85,000**, benefits shall be reduced according to the formula specified in Section 40A.

## Rounding Convention

All monetary calculations shall use **round-half-up** methodology to **2 decimal places**, consistent with Australian taxation standards.

**Note**: This is a demonstration excerpt. The actual Social Security Act 1991 is far more comprehensive. Consult the official Commonwealth legislation for complete details.
